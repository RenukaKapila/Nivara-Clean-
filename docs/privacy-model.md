# Privacy Model

Nivara is designed around a local-first safety model.

## What Stays Local

- Pasted suspicious messages
- Redacted message previews
- Practice progress
- Lesson completion
- Before You Pay answers
- Safe Share templates

## What Users Should Know

Nivara teaches warning signs. It cannot prove whether a message is safe or unsafe. Important situations should be verified through official websites, official apps, known phone numbers, banks, payment providers, or local authorities.

## Data Minimization Rules

- Do not store raw message checks in a backend by default.
- Do not store verification codes, passwords, account numbers, SSNs, or full payment details.
- Prefer aggregate progress over message history.
- If accounts are added later, keep syncing optional.

## Required Disclaimers

Every high-risk flow should keep language like:

> Nivara provides educational warning-sign guidance only. It does not guarantee scam detection and is not legal, financial, banking, law-enforcement, or emergency advice.
